New England Shipwreck Diving

Numerous ships have gone down off the New England Coast during the past couple of centuries. Here, we'll try to tell you more about some of the ones contemporary sport divers visit.

  • Chester A. Poling A favorite dive site off Cape Ann, the stern of this ship is a favorite for many MWDC members. Also included here is a photo from an October 1995 night dive on the Poling.
  • City of Salisbury Steve Simpson compiled several news articles concerning recovery of the massive anchor from this Cape Ann shipwreck. Also included are some photos that document this accomplishment. (Note, this link takes you to Steve's Web site. Please come "back" to the MWDC Web site after you learn about the City of Salisbury.)
  • Shipwrecks of Monomoy Island: During the past two centuries, more than 3,000 ships have gone down in the waters East of Cape Cod. Here you will find some specific information about some of our favorite shipwreck dive sites in that region of coastal Massachusetts.

If you have any suggestions or additions, send them to us at Metrowest Dive Club and we'll try to get them posted ASAP.
